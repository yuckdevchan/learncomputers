# Servers
[Servers](server-(computing)) are computers used for infrastructure and services.

Servers are often used to host websites, databases, video game multiplayer services, and more.

(w) Software services that run on servers are often referred to as 'servers' as well.

They usually run 24/7 and are designed to be reliable and stable. 

Many servers are designed to be rack-mounted in data centers.

Servers can be built from consumer-grade hardware, but many companies choose to use enterprise-grade hardware for better reliability and performance.

Servers often don't need components such as [monitors](monitor), [keyboard](keyboard), [mice](mouse), [speakers](speaker), or [sound cards](sound-card).

Most servers run [Linux](linux) or [BSD](bsd) operating systems, but some run [Windows Server](windows-server). A server version of an [operating system](whats-an-operating-system) often doesn't include a [GUI](gui) to save on resources and improve performance. This is known as '[headless](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Headless_computer)' mode.

## Data centers
A [data center](data-center) is a facility that houses many servers.

### Environmental impact
There are concerns over the [environmental impact](human-impact-on-the-environment) and risk in relation to [climate change](climate-change) of data centers, not just because of [electricity usage](electric-energy-consumption) but also because of heat and water usage.

Data centers use up water because water is very effective at cooling servers. Eventually the water is heated up and is released back into the environment, which can have negative effects on the local ecosystem.

Data center water usage can [significantly impact water availability](https://sustainableict.blog.gov.uk/2025/09/17/ais-thirst-for-water/) for whole communities.

These concerns have been amplified by the increasing number of data centers and their increasing energy usage due to investment in [LLM](llm) and [Cryptocurrency](cryptocurrency) mining servers.
