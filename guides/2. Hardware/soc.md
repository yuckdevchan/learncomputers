# SOC Computers
SOC stands for [System on a Chip](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/System_on_a_chip).

A **system on a chip** is a **single** chip that **contains all** or **most** of the **components** of a **computer**. The **primary chip** usually **includes** the [CPU](cpu), [GPU](gpu), [RAM](ram), [NIC](nic), and other components.

  **SOCs** are **often** used in [mobile devices](mobile) like [Android](android) or [iOS](iOS) [smartphones](smartphone) and [tablets](tablet-computer), or in [embedded systems](embedded-systems).

## Pros
**Each pro** has **exceptions**.
- **Smaller** size
- **Lower** power consumption
- **Fewer** components to **assemble**

## Cons
**Each con** has **exceptions**.
- **More restrictive** in terms of **upgradability** and **repairability**
- **More expensive** to **design**

## Devices that use SOCs:
- Most **smartphones** & **tablets**
- [Laptops](laptop) with [Snapdragon chips](snapdragon) (See [CPU - Architectures](cpu#architectures))
- [Macbook](macbook) **laptops** with [Apple M-Series chips](apple-M-series) (See [Apple Mac](apple-mac))
