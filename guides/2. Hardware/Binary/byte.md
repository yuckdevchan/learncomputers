# Byte
A [byte](byte) is a **unit** of data that is **8 bits** long. A **bit** is a **1** or a **0** (See [binary](binary)). 

(w) In **almost every computer** a byte is **8 bits long**, but it **doesn't have** to be. The word **byte** is **commonly used** to mean **8 bits long**, though. A more **correct term** would be **octet**.<br><br>This **guide** and **other guides** will use **the word byte** to mean **8 bits long**.

## Units
- **1 bit** = 1 or 0
- **1 byte** = 8 bits

**These** are the **metric units** of **bytes**:
- **1 kilobyte** (KB) = 1000 bytes
- **1 megabyte** (MB) = 1000 kilobytes = 1,000,000 bytes
- **1 gigabyte** (GB) = 1000 megabytes = 1,000,000,000 bytes
- **1 terabyte** (TB) = 1000 gigabytes = 1,000,000,000,000 bytes

**These** are the **binary units** of **bytes**:
- **1 kibibyte (KiB)** = 1024 bytes
- **1 mebibyte (MiB)** = 1024 kibibytes = 1,048,576 bytes
- **1 gibibyte (GiB)** = 1024 mebibytes = 1,073,741,824 bytes
- **1 tebibyte (TiB)** = 1024 gibibytes = 1,099,511,627,776 bytes

The **metric units** are more commonly used in **marketing**, while the **binary units** are more commonly used in **technical contexts**.

(w) The [Windows](microsoft-windows) **file explorer** uses the **binary units**, though they are **referred to** using the **names** for the **metric units**. **Many** other **sites** and **applications** **mix up these units** like this due to **differeing conventions**.

**These lists** continue with **petabytes**, **exabytes**, and **zettabytes**, but these are more rarely used because they are **so huge**.

(e) **Google** is **estimated** to store over **15 exabytes** (or **15,000 gigabytes**) of data, and the **entire internet** is **estimated** to be over **40 zettabytes** of data.
