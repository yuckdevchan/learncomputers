# Keyboard
A [keyboard](keyboard) is a [peripheral](peripheral) which allows **a user** to **type** [characters](character%20(symbol)) or **send control commands** to the **computer**.

It can be **used** to **input alphabetical characters**, **numbers**, or **commands** like **arrow keys**, **function keys** (**F1-F12**), and **others**.

(e) Most **keyboards** only have **12 function keys**, but [operating systems](whats-an-operating-system) such as [Windows](microsoft-windows) and [Linux](linux) recognise **F1** up to **F24**!

## Layers & Modifiers
Keyboard have multiple **layers**. For example, if you hold down Shift, uppercase letters are typed instead of lowercase, and symbols instead of numbers.

(e) The **control key** has its **own symbol**, but its **almost never used**. Take a **look** [here](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:ISO_7000_-_Ref-No_2028.svg).
